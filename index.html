ABOUT US

The spirit of driving change

Fiercely committed to service, PECT was established 15 years ago with a burning desire to bridge the gap of opportunities that eludes marginal communities who live in the rural areas of our civilisation. Realising that the effects of poverty can largely be overcome by planting the seeds of education and ensuring access to healthcare and social infrastructure, PECT drives multiple campaigns in far-flung rural areas, with a special focus on women and children.

Galvanising interventions in their age-old beliefs and promoting inclusive dialogue for a healthier way of life, we work along with our steadfast allies, local people's representatives, and professional volunteers to break the cycle of generational destitution in these areas. Together, we believe we can imminently overcome the chasm between the so-called 'less-fortunate' and 'more-fortunate' and move towards an entirely wholesome and fortunate community.

Education

Education is the only way to save girls from a future of being restricted to slaving in the kitchen, with total financial dependence on the men of the household. A large number of girls drop out of schools in their teens because they don’t have access to or can’t afford higher education. Sometimes the reason is simply that their families feel no need for educating their girl children. PECT is on a mission to get girls to learn and lead.

Healthcare

Rural communities in general, are immersed in lifestyles that may cause more harm than good. Since they’re also disconnected from the rest of the world, they are mostly the last to know about current healthcare emergencies.

sanitation

Sanitation facilities are often dismal in rural areas, which can lead to public health issues that cannot be resolved with the limited healthcare resources available. It can also lead to environmental issues and suboptimal productivity and income from agriculture.

Women's Health

The strength of a community lies in possessing infrastructure that promotes economic activity and general wellbeing. From schools and hospitals to parks and public toilets, there are wide-ranging facilities that are necessary, especially in rural areas.

Disaster Relief

Rural women are at the lowest rung when it comes to access to medical care and authentic information regarding their health. PECT team travels to interior areas to educate and empower them to take care of their health. Our team is trained to connect with women from diverse backgrounds and through informative classes and engaging activities, broaden the horizon of their knowledge.

Social infrastructure

In the past 15 years of its existence, PECT has reached out to thousands of people who have had their lives and livelihoods affected by disasters from tsunami and earthquakes to the COVID-19 pandemic.

OUR FOUNDER

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet

Leading PECT, we have Dr Priya Rajendran, a serial entrepreneur whose business ventures encompass the fields of education, media, and philanthropy. Breaking the shackles of convention, she has walked down multiple avenues instead of following a single career path. She graduated from medical school with distinction and trained in Orthopaedics at one of the best hospitals in New York. She is what we like to call a ‘multi-passionate entrepreneur’. One of her profound desires was to reach out to the farthest rungs of society, especially to those women and children who make up the skeleton of the society and yet, are the most excluded from societal development.


Thus began her journey of spearheading large-scale awareness campaigns, healthcare initiatives, medical camps, and donation drives that worked towards uplifting rural societies. Dr Priya believes in the limitless potential of the human race, and in the days to come, wishes to bridge the gaps in opportunities available for urban and rural communities, establishing a singular global community.
